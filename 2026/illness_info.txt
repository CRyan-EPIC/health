ILLNESS INFORMATION
Disease
	Signs
	Symptoms
	Risk Factors
	Incubation Time
	Treatment
	Food Poisoning


	Vomiting, diarrhea
	Stomach cramps, nausea, fever
	Eating spoiled or contaminated food
	1 to 48 hours
	Rest, fluids, avoid high fat or sugar foods
	Head Lice


	Bugs/nits in hair, visible eggs
	Itchy scalp, tickling feeling on head
	Close contact, sharing hats or combs
	Eggs hatch in 7–10 days
	Special shampoos, fine-tooth combing, clean bedding
	Pink Eye   (Conjunctivitis)


	Red, watery eyes, discharge
	Itchy or burning eyes, blurry vision
	Viral/bacterial infection, allergies
	1 to 3 days (viral), up to 5 days (bacterial)
	Eye drops, warm compresses, stay home when contagious
	Allergies




	Runny nose, red eyes, sneezing
	Itchy eyes/nose, coughing, fatigue
	Pollen, dust, pets, mold
	Immediate after exposure
	Antihistamines, avoiding triggers
	Acne 
	Pimples, blackheads, red bumps
	Sore or itchy spots, low self-esteem
	Hormones, oily skin, diet, poor hygiene
	Develops over days to weeks
	Gentle face wash, creams, dermatologist referral
	Eczema


	Dry, red, cracked skin
	Itching, irritation, burning sensation
	Allergies, stress, irritants
	Chronic condition (may flare anytime)
	Moisturizers, steroid creams, avoid triggers
	Flu (Influenza) 
	Fever, chills, body aches
	Tiredness, sore throat, cough
	Close contact with ill people, winter season
	1 to 4 days
	Rest, fluids, antiviral meds (sometimes), flu shot
	Asthma 
	Wheezing, coughing 
	Chest tightness, shortness of breath
	Allergies, exercise, cold air
	Triggered immediately
	Inhalers, avoid triggers, long-term meds
	Strep Throat


	Red/white patches or swelling on tonsils, fever
	Sore throat, headache, stomach ache, body aches
	Close contact, shared drinks
	2 to 5 days
	Antibiotics, rest, fluids
	Common Cold 
	Runny nose, sneezing, mild cough, mild fever
	Sore throat, tiredness, congestion, headache 
	Poor handwashing, being near sick people
	1 to 3 days
	Rest, fluids, over-the-  counter meds
	Type 1 Diabetes


	Frequent peeing, weight loss, fruity smelling breath
Elevated blood sugar
	Thirst, hunger, shaky, fatigue, blurred vision
	Family history and genetics. Some virus can trigger the autoimmune response
	Chronic
Onset common between 4-7 or 10-14 y/o
	Insulin injections and careful tracking of carbohydrates and blood sugar levels
	Caffeine Toxicity
	Fast heart, seizure, diarrhea, sleep changes, muscle twitches, high blood pressure
	Headache, anxiety, thirsty, shortness of breath, confusion, nausea
	Drinking too much caffeine
	Symptoms occur within 1-3 hours after consumption
	Manage symptoms. May need IV fluids, activated charcoal, dialysis, CPR (if your heart stops)

	Clinical Depression
	Tearful, angry outbursts, frustration over small things, altered sleep patterns, weight changes 
	Feeling: sad, hopeless, irritable, tired, anxious, restless, or guilty.
Difficulty concentrating or making decisions, appetite changes , disinterest in hobbies

(outside of a major life change)

Lifestyle changes
	Computer Vision Syndrome
	Eye redness, eye tearing
	Vision changes (double or blurred), dry eyes, eye pain, eye fatigue, sensitivity to light, headaches, neck and shoulder pain
	Must rule out vision impairment first.
Symptoms are worse with screen use (especially long periods of screen use)
	Vision changes can continue occurring even when off screens. Symptoms typically get worse when on screens 
	

Eye drops, modifying screen angles, glare, and contrast. Limit time or increase breaks
	Internet Gaming Disorder
	Withdrawal signs include: aggression or angry outbursts
	Always thinking about gaming, need more time, losing control, losing interest in other things, lying about play time, using game to hide feelings, problems at home or school
	5 symptoms must be present during the same year
	Often enough to cause daily disturbances in your normal activities, relationships, and/or school or social activities
	Individual or family therapy

	Ankle Sprain


	Swelling, bruising, limited movement
	Pain, tenderness, trouble walking
	Sports, jumping/landing wrong, uneven ground
	Rest, ice, compression, elevation (RICE), physical therapy
	Fractured Arm/Wrist 
	Swelling, visible deformity, bruising
	Intense pain, trouble moving arm/wrist
	Falls, contact sports, skateboarding
	Immobilization (cast/splint), x-ray, possible surgery
	Knee Sprain
ACL/MCL Injury  
	Swollen knee, limping, instability
	Pain during movement, stiffness
	Running, twisting motions, sports
	RICE, knee brace, physical therapy
	Dislocated Shoulder 
	Bulge or deformity in shoulder area
	Sharp pain, can't move shoulder, numbness or tingling in fingers/arm
	Falls, sports collisions
	Popping joint back into place (by doctor), sling, rehab
	Clavicle (collar bone) Fracture 
	Swelling in upper chest, deformity in upper chest, bruising in upper chest
	Pain in upper chest, pain when moving arm on injured side 
	One of the most fractured bones in the body. Sports, trauma, falls
	x-ray, pain reliever, ice, elevation with sling, possible surgery
